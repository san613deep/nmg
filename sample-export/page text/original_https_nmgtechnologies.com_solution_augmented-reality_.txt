Technology Stack


    • Language
    • AR SDKs (Software Development Kits)
    • C#
      C# is a widely-used language, particularly within the Unity game engine.
      Its object-oriented nature and strong typing make it well-suited for
      developing complex AR applications.
    • C++
      C++ is a high-performance language often preferred for demanding AR
      applications that require maximum performance and low-level control. We
      utilize C++ within the Unreal Engine, a powerful game engine known for
      its stunning visuals and high-fidelity graphics.
    • Java/Kotlin
      Java and Kotlin are the primary languages used to develop Android AR
      applications. Java's platform independence and Kotlin's concise syntax
      and modern features make them ideal for developing robust and scalable AR
      applications that cater to the diverse Android ecosystem.
    • Swift
      Swift is the primary language for iOS development, making it essential
      for building AR applications on Apple devices. We utilize Swift in
      conjunction with the ARKit framework to create innovative and
      user-friendly AR experiences for iPhone and iPad users.
    • ARKit (Apple)
      ARKit is Apple's proprietary platform for building augmented reality
      experiences on iOS devices. Its advanced features, such as motion
      tracking, scene understanding, and light estimation, allow us to create
      highly immersive and realistic AR experiences.
    • ARCore (Google)
      ARCore is Google's equivalent to ARKit, designed for Android devices. It
      utilizes similar core functionalities to integrate virtual content into
      the real world. We use ARCore to develop cross-platform AR applications
      that reach a broader audience, with compatibility and accessibility
      across different Android devices.
    • Vuforia (PTC)
      Vuforia is a widely used, platform-agnostic AR SDK that supports various
      devices, including iOS, Android, and even smart glasses. It boasts
      advanced features such as image recognition, object recognition and
      tracking, and spatial recognition, which allows us to create complex and
      interactive AR experiences.
    • Wikitude
      Wikitude is another popular cross-platform AR SDK known for its focus on
      real-time image recognition and tracking. It excels in applications like
      visual search, product visualization, and location-based AR experiences.
      Wikitude offers a range of features, including 3D model rendering and GPS
      positioning, that enables us to create engaging and informative AR
      applications.

Our Development Process


Our AR Development Process.

    • 01
      Concept & Design
      Our process begins with a thorough understanding of your project goals
      and vision. We conduct in-depth discussions to gather requirements,
      explore your target audience, and define the core functionalities of your
      AR experience.

      Based on these insights, we create a detailed concept document outlining
      the key features, user flow, and overall user experience. This stage also
      includes initial 3D model creation and prototyping to visualize the AR
      elements and their interactions within the real world.
    • 02
      AR Development & Prototyping
      Next, we use AR SDKs (like ARCore or ARKit), and our development team
      starts building the core AR functionalities. This involves creating
      interactive 3D models, implementing real-time tracking and object
      recognition, and developing the user interface (UI) for interacting with
      the AR content. We iterate on prototypes throughout this stage, gathering
      feedback and refining the AR experience to ensure it meets your
      expectations and provides a seamless user experience.
    • 03
      Integration & Testing
      Once the core AR functionalities are developed, we integrate them into
      the chosen platform (mobile, web, or wearable). This may involve
      integrating with existing applications or building a dedicated AR
      application. Rigorous testing is conducted at every stage, including
      device compatibility testing, performance optimization, and user
      acceptance testing to identify and address any issues before deployment.
    • 04
      Deployment & Launch
      Upon successful completion of testing, we prepare the AR experience for
      deployment. This may involve submitting the application to app stores
      (like Google Play or Apple App Store), configuring cloud services for
      data management, and providing necessary documentation and support for
      ongoing maintenance. We also assist with the launch and marketing of your
      AR experience to ensure it reaches your target audience.
    • 05
      Ongoing Support & Maintenance
      Our commitment extends beyond the initial launch. We provide ongoing
      support and maintenance services, including bug fixes, performance
      optimizations, and updates to incorporate new AR technologies and
      features. We also offer ongoing consultation to help you evolve your AR
      experience, explore new opportunities, and maximize its impact.

FAQ’s.


What are the three types of augmented reality?

Three primary types of AR exist: Location-based AR triggers content based on
the user's location, like virtual road signs appearing on a phone's camera.
Marker AR uses visual markers, such as QR codes, to activate virtual content
when scanned by a device's camera, which is commonly used in advertising and
gaming. Projection AR projects 3D imagery onto surfaces, creating holographic
effects often seen in events.


What are some examples of augmented reality?

The examples include immersive AR games like Pokémon GO, innovative virtual
try-ons for retail, and intuitive AR navigation apps for seamless travel.


What is the difference between VR and AR?

VR fully immerses users in a virtual world, whereas AR overlays interactive
digital elements onto our real-world surroundings.


How does augmented reality work?

Augmented reality (AR) uses advanced cameras and sensors to detect real-world
environments and then overlay interactive digital information on them.
